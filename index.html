SOC interpretation: The SOC curve represents the battery state-of-charge during the representative daylight period. Rising SOC means the battery is charging from excess PV generation above the ramp-limited export target. Falling SOC means the battery is discharging to smooth rapid downward solar ramps and maintain the 3 MW/min POC ramp limit. The SOC profile should vary more aggressively for smaller MWh batteries and remain flatter for larger energy capacities.
Advanced day-selection methodology:
• Worst Ramp Stress Day = highest squared ramp-violation severity after applying the 100 MW POC export limit and 3 MW/min compliance threshold.
• Highest Variability Day = highest detrended volatility using a 15-minute moving-average baseline after POC limiting.
• SOC operating window constrained to 10%–90% to reflect realistic utility-scale BESS operation and degradation management.
Largest SOC Drop Day: 05-07 was identified using cumulative downward ramp violations beyond the 3 MW/min threshold after applying the 100 MW POC export limit. This produces the strongest sustained discharge stress on the BESS and the largest SOC depletion events.
SOC-window-aware cycling: Annual and daily equivalent cycles now dynamically update with the selected SOC operating window. Narrower SOC windows reduce usable energy capacity, increase saturation frequency, reduce achievable throughput, and therefore alter effective cycling behavior and ramp-control capability.
45%-55% Dummy SOC Window: This intentionally restrictive operating range is included as a validation/debugging mode to confirm that throughput, annual cycles, daily cycles, and ramp-control capability dynamically respond to SOC-window constraints. The very small usable energy band should produce substantially lower throughput and visibly different SOC behavior.
